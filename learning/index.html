The 7 Learning Groups of the New World

Click a group to expand. Use this as inspiration — adapt it to your child, your land, and your season.

These groups aren’t grades or rigid programs — they’re living pathways you can adapt to the child, the land, and the rhythm of the season.

Group 1 — The Seed Years Ages 4–6 • The Age of Wonder

Core Themes

  • Awareness of self, body, breath
  • Colors, sounds, simple patterns
  • Connection to nature
  • Emotional grounding
  • Curiosity
  • Basic numeracy and literacy

Subjects

  1. Consciousness Foundations
    • Naming emotions
    • Heart-breath (your practice)
    • Listening to silence
    • Observing without judgment
  2. Nature & Earth Play
    • Planting seeds
    • Tending a small garden bed
    • Feeling textures (soil, leaves, water)
    • “Listening walks”
  3. Early Math & Patterns
    • Shapes, counting, simple patterns
    • Recognizing rhythm in nature
  4. Story & Language
    • Simple storytelling
    • Drawing + symbol-making
    • Letters through play
  5. Creativity & Play
    • Music, movement, dance
    • Clay, painting, crafting
  6. Gentle AI Interaction
    • AI as a storytelling partner
    • AI to draw their ideas
Group 2 — The Sprout Years Ages 7–9 • The Age of Exploration

Core Themes

  • Understanding relationships
  • Noticing patterns
  • Asking questions
  • Taking care of space and things

Subjects

  1. Conscious Awareness
    • Breathing with intention
    • Recognizing ego vs. feelings
    • Cooperative play
  2. Earth & Ecology Basics
    • Soil layers
    • Weather patterns
    • Seasons and moon cycles
    • Planting + caring for 1 “guardian plant”
  3. Applied Math
    • Measuring garden beds
    • Basic geometry through nature
    • Money & simple trade
  4. Storytelling & Language Growth
    • Journaling (short)
    • Mythology from around the world
    • Hero’s journey introduction
  5. Creativity & Design
    • Building simple structures
    • Music rhythms
    • Story-based drawing
  6. AI Literacy (Level 1)
    • Asking questions
    • Identifying useful vs. silly answers
    • AI as a research buddy
Group 3 — The Root Years Ages 10–12 • The Age of Identity

Core Themes

  • Who am I?
  • What do I value?
  • How do systems work?

Subjects

  1. Consciousness & Identity
    • Ego awareness
    • Growth mindset
    • Emotional vocabulary expansion
    • Introduction to shadow work
  2. Earth Systems & Life Science
    • Water cycles
    • Photosynthesis
    • Food chains
    • Garden ecosystem creation
    • Herbal medicine introduction
  3. Practical Math (Real World)
    • Fractions through recipes
    • Geometry through building boxes
    • Basic budgeting
    • Probability through games
  4. Mythology & Human Story
    • Archetypes
    • Tribal stories
    • Symbolic interpretation
    • Story-writing
  5. Creativity & Maker Projects
    • Sewing
    • Woodworking basics
    • Clay vessels
    • Rhythm and drumming
  6. AI Literacy (Level 2)
    • Fact-checking
    • Prompts with intention
    • AI for small projects
Group 4 — The Trunk Years Ages 13–14 • The Age of Integration

Core Themes

  • Seeing systems
  • Understanding community
  • Becoming responsible

Subjects

  1. Conscious Human Development
    • Emotional regulation
    • Empathy training
    • Body intuition
    • Intro to meditation (longer sessions)
  2. Earth & Biology in Practice
    • Soil regeneration
    • Compost
    • Permaculture basics
    • Herbal medicine (safe plants)
  3. Math for Life
    • Ratios, percentages
    • Basic algebra through real scenarios
    • Financial literacy (saving, borrowing, trade)
  4. Systems of the World
    • Cyclical history
    • Power structures
    • Government forms (tribal to modern)
    • Media literacy
  5. Creativity & Innovation
    • Build something real
    • Theater or storytelling performance
    • Music composition basics
  6. AI in Creation
    • AI journals
    • AI-assisted research
    • Early coding logic
Group 5 — The Canopy Years Ages 15–16 • The Age of Vision

Core Themes

  • “What is my path?”
  • Leadership & collaboration
  • Seeing the world with clarity

Subjects

  1. Conscious Leadership & Psychology
    • Group dynamics
    • Conflict mediation
    • Archetypes and personal patterns
    • Ego study
    • Decision making
  2. Applied Earth & Science
    • Regenerative agriculture
    • Water systems
    • Energy systems (solar, wind, alternatives)
    • Anatomy for health and longevity
  3. Math of the Real World
    • Algebra (kept)
    • Statistics + data
    • Probability
    • Practical finance
    • Cryptoeconomics basics
  4. Mythology, Philosophy & Ethics
    • Great human questions
    • Symbolism
    • Meaning-making
    • Ethical dilemmas
  5. Innovation Lab
    • Design thinking
    • Prototype building
    • Community-based solutions
  6. AI Collaboration (Advanced)
    • Automation
    • Using AI as a creative partner
    • Coding fundamentals
Group 6 — The Elder Sapling Years Ages 17–18 • The Age of Mastery

Core Themes

  • Purpose
  • Contribution
  • Responsibility
  • Leadership through service

Subjects

  1. Mastery of Self (Consciousness)
    • Deep meditation
    • Shadow integration
    • Emotional mastery
    • Purpose discovery
  2. Earth Mastery
    • Grow a full garden bed alone
    • Herbal remedies creation
    • Water + soil management
    • Designing a regenerative system
  3. Math for the Future
    • Data science basics
    • Real estate math
    • Business finance
    • Predictive modeling (light)
  4. Human Systems & Future Studies
    • Geopolitics
    • Civilizational cycles
    • Decentralized governance
    • Tribal council models
    • Collapse & renewal cycles (your specialty)
  5. Capstone Innovation Project
    • A product, system, movement, or garden
    • A solution for community well-being
    • An AI tool that supports their work
  6. AI Mastery
    • Project automation
    • Creative production
    • Research
    • Ethical understanding
    • Personal AI assistant creation
Group 7 — The Emerging Elders Post-18 • The Apprenticeship Path

Some return to teach. Some become healers. Some become cultivators. Some become leaders. Some become innovators. They learn to serve and anchor the next generation.

Ready to begin? Start with Group 1 and choose one practice + one outdoor activity this week. Then save what you learn in the Library.
