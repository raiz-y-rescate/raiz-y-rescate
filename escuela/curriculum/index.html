What changes

Instead of separating life into Math / English / Science / History… we teach by domains that match reality, and we measure growth by skills, character, and contribution.

This page is intentionally “bones.” Next we’ll add printable templates, lesson rhythms, and a simple navigation for teachers.

Core Domains (replace traditional subjects)

  • Communication — reading, writing, speaking, languages, storytelling
  • Logic & Systems — math as pattern, reasoning, models, cause/effect
  • Nature & Life — ecology, health, food, energy, climate, observation
  • Human Story — history as cycles, civics, ethics, media literacy
  • Making & Building — hands-on creation, tools, design, repair
  • Digital & AI Literacy — using tools wisely, prompts, verification
  • Self & Relationship — emotional skills, boundaries, community, service
  • Economics & Stewardship — value, trade, budgets, resources, land

Assessment (simple and human)

  • Skills: can the learner do it?
  • Understanding: can they explain it simply?
  • Character: responsibility, courage, kindness, resilience
  • Contribution: did this help the home/community?

Levels (Pre-K → Senior)

    THE 7 LEARNING GROUPS OF THE NEW WORLD

    • Group 1 — The Seed Years (Ages 4–6) — Kindergarten–1st equivalent; the Age of Wonder
      • Core Themes: Awareness of self (body/breath), colors & patterns, connection to nature, emotional grounding, curiosity, basic numeracy & literacy
      • Domains:
        • Consciousness Foundations — naming emotions, heart-breath practice, listening to silence, observing without judgment
        • Nature & Earth Play — planting seeds, tending a small bed, textures (soil/leaves/water), “listening walks”
        • Early Math & Patterns — shapes, counting, simple patterns, rhythm in nature
        • Story & Language — simple storytelling, drawing + symbol-making, letters through play
        • Creativity & Play — music, movement/dance, clay/painting/crafting
        • Gentle AI Interaction — AI as storytelling partner, AI to draw their ideas
    • Group 2 — The Sprout Years (Ages 7–9) — 2nd–3rd equivalent; the Age of Exploration
      • Core Themes: relationships, pattern noticing, questions, caring for space and things
      • Domains:
        • Conscious Awareness — breathing with intention, recognizing ego vs feelings, cooperative play
        • Earth & Ecology Basics — soil layers, weather patterns, seasons & moon cycles, 1 “guardian plant”
        • Applied Math — measuring beds, geometry through nature, money & simple trade
        • Storytelling & Language Growth — short journaling, world mythology, hero’s journey intro
        • Creativity & Design — simple structures, music rhythms, story-based drawing
        • AI Literacy (Level 1) — asking questions, useful vs silly answers, AI as research buddy
    • Group 3 — The Root Years (Ages 10–12) — 4th–6th equivalent; the Age of Identity
      • Core Themes: who am I, what do I value, how systems work
      • Domains:
        • Consciousness & Identity — ego awareness, growth mindset, emotional vocabulary, intro to shadow work
        • Earth Systems & Life Science — water cycles, photosynthesis, food chains, garden ecosystem, herbal intro
        • Practical Math (Real World) — fractions via recipes, geometry via building, budgeting, probability via games
        • Mythology & Human Story — archetypes, tribal stories, symbolism, story-writing
        • Creativity & Maker Projects — sewing, woodworking basics, clay vessels, rhythm/drumming
        • AI Literacy (Level 2) — fact-checking, prompts with intention, AI for small projects
    • Group 4 — The Trunk Years (Ages 13–14) — 7th–8th equivalent; the Age of Integration
      • Core Themes: seeing systems, understanding community, responsibility
      • Domains:
        • Conscious Human Development — emotional regulation, empathy, body intuition, longer meditation
        • Earth & Biology in Practice — soil regeneration, compost, permaculture basics, safe herbal plants
        • Math for Life — ratios/percentages, algebra via real scenarios, saving/borrowing/trade
        • Systems of the World — cyclical history, power structures, governance forms, media literacy
        • Creativity & Innovation — build something real, performance/storytelling, music composition basics
        • AI in Creation — AI journals, AI-assisted research, early coding logic
    • Group 5 — The Canopy Years (Ages 15–16) — 9th–10th equivalent; the Age of Vision
      • Core Themes: “what is my path?”, leadership & collaboration, clarity
      • Domains:
        • Conscious Leadership & Psychology — group dynamics, conflict mediation, archetypes, decision making
        • Applied Earth & Science — regenerative agriculture, water systems, energy systems, anatomy for longevity
        • Math of the Real World — algebra (kept), statistics/data, probability, finance, cryptoeconomics basics
        • Mythology, Philosophy & Ethics — great questions, symbolism, meaning-making, ethical dilemmas
        • Innovation Lab — design thinking, prototyping, community solutions
        • AI Collaboration (Advanced) — automation, AI as creative partner, coding fundamentals
    • Group 6 — The Elder Sapling Years (Ages 17–18) — 11th–12th equivalent; the Age of Mastery
      • Core Themes: purpose, contribution, responsibility, leadership through service
      • Domains:
        • Mastery of Self (Consciousness) — deep meditation, shadow integration, emotional mastery, purpose discovery
        • Earth Mastery — manage a full bed, herbal remedies, water/soil management, regenerative design
        • Math for the Future — data science basics, real estate math, business finance, light predictive modeling
        • Human Systems & Future Studies — geopolitics, civilizational cycles, decentralized governance, collapse & renewal
        • Capstone Innovation Project — build a product/system/movement/garden; a community solution; an AI tool to support it
        • AI Mastery — automation, creative production, research, ethics, personal AI assistant creation
    • Group 7 — The Emerging Elders (Post-18) — The Apprenticeship Path
      • Path: Some return to teach. Some become healers. Some cultivate. Some lead. Some innovate.
      • Purpose: Service, anchoring, and guiding the next generation.

Monthly Rhythm (teacher-friendly)

  • Week 1: Choose a theme (Water / Food / Shelter / Story / Health / Energy)
  • Week 2: Learn + practice (2 domains)
  • Week 3: Build something real (project)
  • Week 4: Share + reflect (portfolio + contribution)
